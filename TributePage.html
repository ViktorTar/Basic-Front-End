Claud Shannon's life time line:

  • 1916 - born in Petoskey, Michigan
  • 1932 - graduated from Gaylord High School entered the University of Michigan, where he took a course that introduced him to the work of George Boole
  • 1936 - graduated with two bachelor's degrees, in electrical engineering and mathematics, began his graduate studies in electrical engineering at MIT
  • 1937 - wrote his master's degree thesis, "A Symbolic Analysis of Relay and Switching Circuits"
  • 1940 - received his Ph.D. degree from MIT, became a National Research Fellow at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, New Jersey
  • 1942 - Shannon is credited with the invention of signal-flow graphs
  • 1943 - came into contact with the leading British mathematician Alan Turing.
  • 1945 - as the war was coming to an end, the NDRC was issuing a summary of technical reports as a last step prior to its eventual closing down. Inside the volume on fire control a special essay titled Data Smoothing and Prediction in Fire-Control Systems, coauthored by Shannon, Ralph Beebe Blackman, and Hendrik Wade Bode, formally treated the problem of smoothing the data in fire-control by analogy with "the problem of separating a signal from interfering noise in communications systems."
  • 1948 - "A Mathematical Theory of Communication", an article in two parts in the July and October issues of the Bell System Technical Journal
  • 1949 - proved that the cryptographic one-time pad is unbreakable in his classified research
  • 1956 - joined the MIT faculty to work in the Research Laboratory of Electronics (RLE)
  • 2001 - died at the age of 84
